Upcoming Events: Two-Part Series

Behind the Stream: How UT Scholars Are Rethinking Music, Labor & Artist Sustainability

Part 1: January 14th, 2026

Capital Factory in Austin, TX • ACL Room, 16th floor

Streaming transformed access to music, but it also obscured the labor, rights, and economic realities behind it. This two-part Prosper XO Town Hall series brings leading University of Texas scholars into conversation with the Austin creative community to examine music not just as art, but as work.

Guest Speakers

Sonia Seeman - Associate Professor, Ethnomusicology, Butler School of Music + Center for Middle Eastern Studies

Eric Drott - Professor of Theory, Virginia L. Murchison Regents Professor in Fine Arts

RSVP for Part 1

Part 2: February 11th, 2026

Capital Factory in Austin, TX • ACL Room, 16th floor

Part Two centers Austin itself, a global "music city" grappling with the realities of artist precarity. This Town Hall highlights research connected to the Support Austin Musicians project and broader scholarship on music, labor, and cultural policy.

Guest Speakers

Robin Moore - Professor of Ethnomusicology and Director of the Center for American Music, Butler School of Music, UT Austin

Charles Carson - Associate Professor of Musicology and Editor of the Journal of the Society for American Music, Butler School of Music, UT Austin

For more information about research conducted by these professors on the challenges Austin musicians face, visit SupportAustinMusicians.org

Resource of the Month:
Austin Texas Musicians

Austin Texas Musicians is committed to elevating industry conditions and empowering professional musicians to lend their voice for change. Founded by musician Nakia Reynoso and established as a nonprofit in 2019, Austin Texas Musicians Fund now represents more than 6,000 working artists across Central Texas, advocating for fair pay, sustainable policies, and long-term support for Austin's music community.

Through advocacy, education, and economic development, the organization is helping ensure musicians can continue to live, work, and thrive in the city they help define. Learn more about their work and how to get involved.
